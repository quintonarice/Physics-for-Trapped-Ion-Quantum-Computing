Laser-Ion Interaction: Coupling Internal States to Motion

The Lamb-Dicke Parameter

Consider the general form of the propagating electric field of a laser:

\[ \ \mathbf{E} (\mathbf{r},t) \propto e^{i(\mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{r} - \omega t + \phi_0)} \tag{4.1} \]

where $\mathbf{k}$ is the wave vector, $\mathbf{r}$ is the position vector, $\omega$ is the laser frequency, and $\phi_0$ is the phase. We start by considering axial coupling only which means that $\mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{r} = kz$. And since we know that in quantum mechanics the position is an operator, $z \rightarrow \hat Z$, then:

\[ \ \mathbf{E} (\hat Z,t) \propto e^{i(k_z \hat Z - \omega t)} \tag{4.2} \]

where we have dropped the phase $\phi_0$. Now, for a charge distribution in an external electric field, the interaction energy is given from classical electromagnetism:

\[ \ U = - \mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{E} \tag{4.3} \]

where $\mathbf{p}$ is the electric dipole moment. Since the electron cloud surrounding the atom moves in response to the external electric field we express the dipole moment as $\mathbf{p} = -e \mathbf{r}_e$ where $e$ is the charge and $\mathbf{r}_e$ is the electrons position relative to the nucleus. In quantum mechanics, the dipole moment becomes an operator acting on the internal electronic states, while the electric field must be evaluated at the ion’s position operator. The resulting interaction Hamiltonian therefore couples the ion’s internal states to its quantized motion:

\[ \ \hat H_{int} = - \mathbf{\hat p} \cdot \mathbf{E}_0 e^{i(k_z \hat Z - \omega t)} \tag{4.4} \]

known as the interaction Hamiltonian where $\mathbf{E}_0$ is the electric field amplitude. Now recall the expression for the position operator $\hat Z$ from the last section.

\[ \ \hat Z = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega_z}} \big (\hat a + \hat a^\dagger) \tag{4.5} \]

So that:

\[ \ \hat H_{int} = - \mathbf{\hat p} \cdot \mathbf{E}_0 e^{i \big (k_z \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega_z}} \big (\hat a + \hat a^\dagger) - \omega t)} \tag{4.6} \]

We now define the Lamb-Dicke parameter as:

\[ \ \eta_z \equiv k_z \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega_z}} \tag{4.7} \]

In what's known as the Lamb-Dicke regime the ion's spatial extent is much smaller than the laser wavelength $\lambda$ and since $k_z \sim \frac{1}{\lambda}$, it follows that in the Lamb-Dicke regime:

\[ \ \eta_z \ll 1 \tag{4.8} \]

Under this approximation, we may expand the exponential term of the interaction Hamiltonian as:

\[ \ e^{i \eta_z (\hat a + \hat a^\dagger) \approx 1 + i \eta_z (\hat a + \hat a^\dagger) + \mathscr O (\eta_z^2) \tag{4.9} \]
